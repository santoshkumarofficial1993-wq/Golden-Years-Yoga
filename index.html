Why gentle yoga works so well later in life

You don't need to touch your toes. A few minutes of mindful movement most days can change how you feel, move and sleep — safely.

Better balance, fewer falls

Standing and seated balance work strengthens the small stabiliser muscles in your ankles, hips and core — the ones that keep you steady on stairs, in the garden and on uneven ground.

  • Steadier on your feet
  • More confidence walking

Looser, more comfortable joints

Slow, supported movement gently lubricates stiff knees, hips and shoulders and eases the morning ache of arthritis. Range of motion improves without strain or impact.

  • Less morning stiffness
  • Easier reaching & bending

Calmer mind, deeper sleep

Pairing breath with movement calms the nervous system. Readers tell us they feel less anxious, more present, and fall asleep more easily after an evening wind-down practice.

  • Lower stress
  • Better rest

Get started in three simple steps

No equipment to buy and nothing to download. Here's exactly how to begin today.

  1. 1

    Find a sturdy chair

    Choose a firm, armless chair on a non-slip floor. Wear comfortable clothes and keep water nearby. That's your whole studio.

  2. 2

    Move for ten minutes

    Follow our seated warm-up and two or three poses. Move slowly, never into pain, and rest whenever you need to.

  3. 3

    Breathe and repeat

    Finish with slow breathing and a moment of rest. Come back most days — small, steady practice is what changes how you feel.

Your questions, answered

I'm not flexible at all — can I really do yoga?

Absolutely. Flexibility is a result of practice, not a requirement to begin. Every pose here has a seated or chair-supported version, and you only ever move within your own comfortable range.

Is it safe with arthritis or after surgery?

Gentle yoga is widely recommended for joint health, but everybody is different. Please read our health disclaimer and check with your doctor or physiotherapist before starting any new exercise — especially after surgery or with a medical condition.

How much time does it take?

As little as ten minutes a day makes a real difference. Consistency matters far more than length — a short daily practice beats an occasional long one.

Do I need special equipment?

No. A sturdy chair and comfortable clothes are enough. A yoga mat and a cushion are nice once you're settled in, but never required to start.
