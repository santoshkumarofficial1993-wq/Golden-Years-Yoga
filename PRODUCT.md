# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults aged roughly 60+ (and the adult children / caregivers who help them get
started). Often new or returning to exercise, frequently managing stiffness,
arthritis, balance concerns, limited mobility, or low vision. They use the site
at home, usually on a tablet, large phone, or desktop, often in good light but
with reading glasses. Their context is cautious curiosity: "Is this safe for
*me*? Can I actually do it? Where do I begin?" The job to be done is to feel
confident that gentle, senior-appropriate yoga is achievable, find a safe place
to start (especially chair-supported movement), and trust the source.

## Product Purpose

Golden Years Yoga is a content-led marketing site that introduces seniors to
gentle, accessible yoga — with a strong emphasis on chair yoga, balance, and
joint-friendly movement. It exists to lower the intimidation barrier to
starting, deliver genuinely useful original guidance, and build enough trust to
convert visitors into newsletter subscribers / program starters. Success looks
like: a senior visitor reads, feels reassured, tries a pose, and signs up.
The site is also built to be monetizable via Google Ads — so it must clear
AdSense/Google Ads policy: substantial original content, clear navigation,
and complete legal/policy pages.

## Brand Personality

Reassuring, warm, and capable. Three words: **encouraging, clear, trustworthy.**
Voice speaks *with* seniors as capable adults — never patronizing ("you've got
this," not "be careful, dear"). Calm but vital, not sleepy. Emotional goals:
replace anxiety with confidence; replace "I'm too old/stiff" with "I can start
today, exactly as I am."

## Anti-references

- The lavender / pastel / lotus-mandala wellness cliché. No incense-shop kitsch.
- Hyper-athletic, bendy-20-something studio yoga imagery that alienates seniors.
- Patronizing "elderly care" beige-and-grey medical-brochure design.
- Thin affiliate/AdSense content farms with a wall of ads and no real substance.
- Tiny low-contrast "elegant" grey type — actively hostile to this audience.

## Design Principles

1. **Legibility is a feature, not a constraint.** Large type, high contrast,
   generous spacing and tap targets are first-class design decisions here.
   Built on the Atkinson Hyperlegible typeface (designed for low vision).
2. **Confidence over caution-theater.** Encourage action. Safety notes are
   present and honest, but framed as empowerment, not fear.
3. **Show the real body in the room.** Imagery centers seniors and
   chair-supported movement — original, owned illustration, never broken stock.
4. **Earn trust to earn the ad.** Real, helpful, original content first; ads
   are clearly labeled, sparse, and never disguised as content or navigation.
5. **Calm, not asleep.** Motion breathes (literally) but never distracts;
   every motion has a reduced-motion alternative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA and beyond for this audience. Body text ≥18px with ≥7:1
contrast; large targets (≥48px); visible focus rings; skip-to-content link;
semantic landmarks and headings; `prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere;
native `<details>` for FAQ; forms fully labeled. Color is never the only signal.
An accessibility statement page is published.
