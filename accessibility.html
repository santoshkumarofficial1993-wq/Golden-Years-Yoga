Accessibility statement

We design for older eyes, hands and devices first — because that's who we're here for.

Golden Years Yoga is committed to making our website usable and welcoming for everyone, including people who are older or living with vision, hearing, mobility or cognitive differences. We aim to meet the WCAG 2.1 Level AA standard, and in several areas we go further for our audience.

What we've built in

  • Large, high-contrast text. Our body text is set in Atkinson Hyperlegible, a typeface designed for low-vision readers, at a generous size with strong contrast.
  • Clear focus indicators. Keyboard users see a bold outline on whatever they're about to click.
  • Big touch targets. Buttons and links are large and well-spaced for less precise taps and clicks.
  • Keyboard friendly. You can navigate the whole site without a mouse, and skip straight to the main content.
  • Reduced motion. If your device requests reduced motion, our gentle animations turn off automatically.
  • Descriptive text. Images carry written descriptions for screen-reader users.
  • Readable layout. Lines of text are kept short, with plenty of spacing.

Adjusting your own device

You can also make the site easier to read using your browser and device. Press Ctrl and + (or Cmd and + on a Mac) to zoom in, and explore your device's built-in accessibility settings for larger text, higher contrast and screen reading.

We're not finished

Accessibility is ongoing work. If you find something hard to read or use, please tell us — your feedback directly improves the site.

Contact us

Email contact@goldenyearsyoga.com and we'll do our best to help, and to provide information in another format if you need it.
