It is never too late to feel better in your body.

Golden Years Yoga exists to make gentle, genuinely safe movement available to every older adult — whatever your starting point, history or mobility.

Our story

Golden Years Yoga started at a kitchen table. After a fall left her nervous about moving, our founder's mother was told to "just take it easy" — advice that left her stiffer, less steady and increasingly afraid of her own home. What actually helped was the opposite: small, gentle, supported movement, done little and often, in a chair she trusted.

We could not find a single resource that spoke to older beginners with respect — large enough to read, slow enough to follow, and honest about safety without drowning everything in fear. So we built one.

What we believe

We believe older adults are capable, not fragile. We believe clear instructions are an act of kindness. And we believe the goal of yoga later in life is not to perform — it is to keep doing the everyday things you love, comfortably and independently, for as long as possible.

Plain and simple: everything we publish is designed to be readable in large print, followed at your own pace, and adapted to a chair. If a movement does not feel right for your body, it is always okay to skip it.

How we keep it safe

  • Supported first. Every routine offers a seated or chair-assisted option before any standing or floor work.
  • Range over depth. We coach moving within a comfortable range, never forcing or "pushing through" pain.
  • Honest guidance. We are an educational resource, not a substitute for medical care. We encourage everyone to speak with their doctor first — see our health disclaimer.
  • Built for real bodies. Arthritis, replaced joints, limited vision and low stamina are the starting points we design for, not exceptions.

Who it's for

Whether you are 60 and newly retired, 75 and rebuilding after surgery, or 85 and simply want to stay steady on your feet — and whether you have practised before or have never set foot in a studio — you belong here. Adult children and caregivers helping a parent get started are very welcome too.

Our promise to you

  • Always free to start
  • Large-print friendly
  • Never patronising
  • No pressure, ever
